Smoke dances lazily in the 
Blonded rays of sunlight
That glisten in my eyes as
I lay on a blanket of green.

A bellowing blue voice projects 
Through the microphone as 
Fingers pluck away at brass strings. 

Strangers walk by, ne’er a care 
To admire the hint of green through the 
Cracks of the concrete.

I shuffle my feet to the vibrations 
And slowly begin to smile.




The tempo slows, and the 
Brass stops sounding.
Talks turn into whispers and 
Smoke clears the air.
Laced with dim light, silhouettes stare.

I lean into it. 

Filled with warmth, giddy
With delight, despite the 
Cold that bruises my hands.

My face runs red,
But you won’t see,
As the dark engulfs the green.
